Project Lifecycle



Requirements Analysis: The first step, involving gathering and analyzing the requirements of the project. This stage ensures that the project team understands the client's needs and goals.

UI and UX Design: After requirements are clear, the focus shifts to user interface (UI) and user experience (UX) design. This step involves designing the system's look and feel, ensuring it's user-friendly and meets the client's expectations.

Architecture Design: The architecture design phase outlines the technical structure and design of the system, deciding on frameworks, tools, and system components needed for front-end and back-end development.

Development (Front-End and Back-End): The Front-End and Back-End development will happen side by side.
Front-End Development: Focuses on building the user-facing part of the system (UI).
Back-End Development: Focuses on the server-side logic, databases, and application functionalities.

Integration: This stage involves combining the front-end and back-end systems to ensure they work together seamlessly. It ensures that all components interact as expected.

Testing and QA: After integration, testing and quality assurance (QA) take place. This involves identifying bugs, ensuring the system functions correctly, and verifying it meets the specified requirements.

Help Desk: Post-development, the help desk provides support to users, helping them troubleshoot any issues they encounter during use.

Continuous Support & Evolution: This stage emphasizes continuous monitoring, support, and improvements based on user feedback and evolving project needs.
